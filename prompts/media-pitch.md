# Prompt: Media Pitch Email Generator

**Copy and paste this prompt into Claude. Fill in the [brackets] with your details.**

---

```
You are a media relations specialist who pitches journalists daily and has a strong track record of earned media placements. Write a concise, compelling journalist pitch for the following announcement.

PITCH DETAILS:
- Company: [e.g., DesertSolar Energy]
- Announcement: [e.g., $4.2M Series A funding to expand residential solar in Nevada and Arizona]
- Target outlet type: [e.g., local business journal / tech media / industry trade pub / national consumer press]
- Target journalist beat: [e.g., clean energy, small business, real estate, tech startups]
- News hook (why NOW): [e.g., Nevada just passed SB 123 expanding solar tax credits; energy costs hit 10-year high]
- Availability: [e.g., CEO available for interview this week; embargo ends April 15]
- Exclusive offer: [yes/no — if yes, describe: e.g., exclusive data on Nevada solar adoption rates]

Generate the following:

---

SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS (3 variants):

Subject A — Data-led: [number or stat + news hook]
Subject B — Story-led: [human angle or narrative hook]  
Subject C — Direct: [announcement + why it matters to their readers]

Keep all subject lines under 60 characters.

---

PITCH EMAIL BODY:

[First name],

[HOOK PARAGRAPH — 2-3 sentences]
- Open with the news hook or trend, NOT with the company name
- Connect to something the journalist has recently covered or their beat
- Make them feel: "this is exactly what my readers care about right now"

[ANNOUNCEMENT PARAGRAPH — 2-3 sentences]
- Now introduce the company and the announcement
- Lead with the most newsworthy fact (funding amount, user milestone, market impact)
- One specific data point that makes this concrete

[WHY YOUR READERS CARE — 2 sentences]
- Explicit connection: "Your readers — [audience] — are dealing with [problem]. [Company] data/story shows [insight/solution]."

[OFFER PARAGRAPH — 2-3 sentences]
- What you're offering: interview with [Name, Title], exclusive data, embargo access
- Availability and flexibility
- "Happy to send the full release, data sheets, or set up a call at your convenience."

[SIGN-OFF]
[Your name]
[Title]
[Company]
[Phone]
[Email]

---

STYLE RULES:
- Total email body: 150-200 words (journalists delete long pitches)
- No attachments mentioned (offer to send)
- No "I hope this email finds you well" or similar filler
- Specific beats and outlet references increase reply rate — use the journalist's recent work if known
- The hook must be about THEIR readers, not about the company

Output all 3 subject lines + full pitch email body.
```
