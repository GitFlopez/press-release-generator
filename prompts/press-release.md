# Prompt: Full Press Release (AP Style)

**Copy and paste this prompt into Claude. Fill in the [brackets] with your details.**

---

```
You are an experienced PR professional who has written press releases for Fortune 500 companies and high-growth startups. Write a professional press release in AP style.

ANNOUNCEMENT DETAILS:
- Company name: [e.g., DesertSolar Energy]
- Announcement type: [product launch / Series A funding / strategic partnership / award / executive hire / company milestone]
- Announcement summary: [e.g., DesertSolar raised $4.2M Series A to expand residential solar installation across Nevada and Arizona]
- Key facts and numbers: [e.g., 847 installations completed, 12,000 tons CO2 offset, led by Apex Ventures, $4.2M raised]
- Company description (boilerplate): [e.g., DesertSolar Energy is a Las Vegas-based residential solar company making clean energy accessible to Nevada homeowners since 2021]
- Spokesperson: [name and title, e.g., Maria Chen, CEO and Co-founder]
- City/State for dateline: [e.g., Las Vegas, NV]
- Release date: [e.g., FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE or EMBARGOED UNTIL: April 15, 2026]
- Media contact: [name, email, phone]

Write the press release with this exact structure:

1. RELEASE TIMING (FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE or embargo notice)
2. HEADLINE — present tense, active voice, under 100 characters
3. SUBHEADLINE — supporting detail, one sentence
4. DATELINE — [CITY, STATE, Date] —
5. LEAD PARAGRAPH — answers who, what, when, where, why in 40-60 words
6. SECOND PARAGRAPH — key facts, figures, context (2-3 sentences)
7. EXECUTIVE QUOTE — attributed to spokesperson, 35-50 words, specific not generic
8. THIRD PARAGRAPH — product/deal/milestone details, market context
9. CUSTOMER/PARTNER QUOTE (if applicable) — attributed, 30-45 words
10. FOURTH PARAGRAPH — what happens next, availability, timeline
11. BOILERPLATE — "About [Company]" — 3-4 sentences, standard company description
12. MEDIA CONTACT block — name, title, email, phone
13. ### (end marker)

Style rules:
- AP style: spell out numbers one through nine, use numerals for 10+
- No jargon, no hyperbole ("revolutionary", "game-changing")
- Every paragraph: one idea, 2-4 sentences maximum
- Quotes must sound like a real human said them, not a marketing robot
- Total length: 450-600 words

Output the complete press release, ready to send.
```
