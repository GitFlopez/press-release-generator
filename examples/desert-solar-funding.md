## OUTPUT 1: Full Press Release

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

**DesertSolar Energy Closes $4.2M Series A to Bring Affordable Solar to Nevada and Arizona Working Families**

*Apex Ventures-led round fuels expansion to Phoenix and Tucson markets as average Nevada homeowner saves $1,847 annually on energy bills*

LAS VEGAS, NV, April 15, 2026 — DesertSolar Energy, a Las Vegas-based residential solar company, today announced the close of a $4.2 million Series A funding round led by Apex Ventures. The capital will accelerate expansion into the Phoenix and Tucson markets in Q3 2026, targeting underserved middle-income homeowners who have been priced out of traditional solar programs.

Since its founding in 2021, DesertSolar has completed 847 residential installations across Nevada, offsetting 12,000 tons of CO2 — the equivalent of removing 2,600 cars from the road for a year. The company's low-barrier financing model reduces upfront installation costs to zero for qualified homeowners, with average annual savings of $1,847 on electricity bills.

"Nevada homeowners are paying some of the fastest-rising electricity rates in the country, and the families who need relief the most have historically been locked out of solar because of high upfront costs," said Maria Chen, CEO and Co-founder of DesertSolar. "This round lets us scale the model that's already working for 847 Las Vegas families into two of the fastest-growing metros in the Sun Belt."

The Arizona expansion targets the Phoenix metro area first, where residential electricity rates average $0.14 per kWh — 23% above the national average — and Tucson, where solar irradiance levels rank among the highest in the United States. DesertSolar plans to hire 40 installation technicians and three regional sales managers in the new markets by Q4 2026.

"DesertSolar's financing model solves the adoption barrier that has stalled residential solar penetration in working-class neighborhoods for a decade," said David Park, Managing Partner at Apex Ventures. "The unit economics are proven in Nevada. Arizona is a natural next step, and the timing with state incentive programs is right."

The company expects to complete its 1,500th Nevada installation by June 2026 and reach 500 Arizona installations within 12 months of market entry. Homeowners interested in the Phoenix or Tucson waitlist can register at desertsolar.com/arizona.

**About DesertSolar Energy**
DesertSolar Energy is a Las Vegas-based residential solar company making clean energy accessible to working-class Nevada homeowners since 2021. Through zero-down financing and a local installation workforce, DesertSolar has completed 847 installations and offset 12,000 tons of CO2 across the Las Vegas metro area. The company is expanding to Arizona in Q3 2026. Learn more at desertsolar.com.
